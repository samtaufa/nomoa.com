Tonga's SchoolNET Project Proposal

Introduction.

The developments in computer education, access at various schools in Tonga provides a prime environment for developing Information Systems that we propose will contribute to the services available to administrators, staff, and students.

The following are a phases towards information dissemination.

  • Staff Training
  • Electronic Archiving
  • Student Records
  • Time Tabling

The proposal is for interested parties to get together, select project leaders, and pursue the creation of solutions beneficial to the individual school systems, and students.

The above noted projects articulate the objectives towards improvements and sustainability of student services through the empowerment with information skills.

Brief of Project Items

Staff Training.

Without digging into references, is a significant cause of failure for the introduction of things including technology. The spread of technology tools at schools such as Liahona, QSC, St. Andrews, Tonga H/C, Tupou H/C, has been held back not by the archaic equipment but the fear of staff members and lack of defining skills. For any success to be long term, staff aclimatisation and integration with the use of technologies is a necessity.  

Electronic Archiving (EA)

EA is now feasible for a number of education systems. The proposal is to design a consistent, transferable, system for archiving data such that it can be reused.  

Student Records

A consistent problem in each school year. The issue is problematic for the clerical staff assessing fee payments, administrators reviewing attendance and parental records, staff members reviewing student progress, and students wishing to know their progress. The proposal is to develop an Information System that can scale to incorporate many of the needed data items now used by various pressure groups.  

Time Tabling

An annual problem for administrators, a significant problem for students locked out of desirable classes due to limited time tabling facilities. Primarily a computing/algorithm exercise the proposal is to develop a single system for all involved schools that can be enhanced by the requirements of different school system.

We seek an elegant, effective solution, that can be implemented across different environments and systems.  

Recommendations

Although it may not seem appropriate, we should consider strongly the involvement of the school community in the design and development of the system. Not only is this necessary to the acceptance of any developed system, but will also be necessary as we must transition maintenance of the system design to the school community.

The above is only a short look at what is needed at the schools in Tonga. Development members may choose a different list and prioritisation.
